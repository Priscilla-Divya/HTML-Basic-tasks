Paneer Butter Masala Recipe (Restaurant Style)

Paneer Butter Masala Recipe is one of India’s most popular paneer preparation. This restaurant style recipe with soft paneer cubes dunked in a creamy, lightly spiced tomato sauce or gravy is a best one that I have been making for a long time. This rich dish is best served with roti or chapati, paratha, naan or rumali roti.

Preparation Time

40 minutes | preparetion + Cooking

Cuisine

North Indian | Main Course

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Step1: Finely chop the onions and tomatoes. Soak the cashew nuts in water.

  2. Step2: Heat some oil in a pan, and add the chopped onions.

    3. Step3: Cook till they are tender. As soon as the onions turn translucent, add the chopped tomatoes.
    Step4: Add salt, and mix well.
  3. Step5: Close the lid, and cook for 3 minutes on a low flame.

  4. Step6: Open the lid, and add coriander powder, chili powder, garam masala, and cashew nuts.

  5. Step7: Stir well, cook till the juices start to disappear, and switch off.

  6. Step8: Let it cool. Transfer the onion and tomato mixture to a blender, add a cup of water, and grind to a fine paste.

  7. Step9: This is the "masala" of our paneer butter masala. Add more water if the masala it's too thick.

  8. Step10: Heat a pan, and melt the butter.

  9. Step11: Fry the dry spices, bay leaf, cardamom, cloves and cinnamon, for 1 minute on a low flame.

  10. Step12: Crush the ginger and garlic, and add to the pan.

  11. Step13: Add the masala to the pan, add more chili powder (if required) for the colour, and mix well.

  12. Step14: Cook with the lid closed for 3 minutes.

  13. Step15: Open the lid, and cook for some more time if you want to reduce the gravy. Add the paneer cubes and the Kasuri methi leaves.

  14. Step16: Add some cream and mix well.

  15. Step17: Garnish with coriander leaves and a pinch of methi leaves. Serve hot with roti or naan.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer (cottage cheese)
  • 3 onions
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 10 Cashew nuts
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 4 - 5 pods garlic
  • 1/2 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon kashmiri chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3 cloves
  • 3 cardamom
  • 1 inch cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi leaves (dry fenugreek)
  • 2 - 3 sprigs coriander leaves
  • To taste salt
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons fresh cream

What to eat with Paneer Butter Masala?

1. Eat with the staple Indian bread roti: Paneer butter masala gravy has a slightly sweet taste and very mildly spiced. Thus making it an excellent gravy to go with roti or chapati.

2. With other Indian breads: Another great combination for this dish is naan bread or tandoori roti or paratha or roomali roti or Malabar paratha.

3. With rice: It also goes well with steamed basmati rice or jeera rice (cumin rice).

FAQs

Are the cashews raw or roasted?

In Indian recipes where cashews are ground to a paste, raw cashews are always used.

Can I use canned tomato purée?

Yes, you can use canned tomato purée, but add up to a teaspoon of sugar in the gravy if the purée is too tangy.

Will blanching tomatoes before puréeing reduce cooking time and will the taste change?

Yes this will reduce the cooking time, but the taste will be same
